Thanks for visiting my home page. I am a working as a Senior Scientist in the research area of computational biology. My current research is focused on to understand the role of two chromatin remodellers Chd4 and Brg1 in regulating promoter activity during stem cell differentiation. I use RNA-seq and ChIP-seq data to study how the binding of the two remodellers alters the promoters landscape.

During my post-doctoral work, I studied the role of two transcription factors (TF), PU.1 and CEBPA during differentiation of stem cells into mature blood cells (hematopoiesis). In contrast to our previous understanding, I showed that CEBPA controls PU.1 levels and loss of CEBPA leads to a very early differentiation block during hematopoiesis. I also showed that formation of granulocytes is driven by a sub-set of enhancers where CEBPA acts a pioneering factor, and are thus not dependent on PU.1 for their activation [PMID: 29847803].

I did my Ph.D. (computational biology) in 2013 under the supervision of Prof. Jan Gorodkin at the University of Copenhagen. I developed and used computational and statistical methods to analyze RNA-seq data for the prediction of non-coding RNAs [PMID: 22053076, 23874353, 24797614, 26042150] and to understand regulation in their post-transcriptional processing [PMID: 26166713].
